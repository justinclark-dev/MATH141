2. Cell Phone Company

A cell phone company charges a flat rate of $50 per month for up to 2GB of data. If you use more than 2GB of data, you will pay an extra $10 for each GB of data used beyond the included 2GB. For example, if you use 6GB of data, you will pay the base at rate of $50, plus $40 ($10 for each of the 4 extra GB of data used), for a total of $90.

  1. How much would your monthly bill be if you use 0.5GB of data? What about 1GB of data? What about 2GB of data?
    Since with each instance the data usage is either 2GB or less, the bill would simply be the flat rate of $50: \[ 0.5GB = $50\] \[ 1GB = $50\] \[ 2GB = $50\]
  2. Use your answers to (a) to find a function \(f(x)\) that gives the cost of your monthly bill if you use \(x\) GB of data, where \(0\le x \le2\) (in other words, you only use 0 to 2GB of data.)
    This is the function for the flat rate cost: \[ f(x) = 50\]
  3. How much would your monthly bill be if you use 3GB of data? What about 4GB of data? What about 5GB of data?
    With \( \color{red}{3GB} \) of data usage we subtract the flat rate of 2GB from the 3GB used which gives us a difference of 1GB: \[ \color{red}{3GB}\\ - 2GB = 1GB \] Then multiply the difference of 1 by the extra GB usage rate of $10: \[ \color{red}{1}\\ \cdot 10 = $10 \] Which gives us a product of \(\color{red}{$10}\\ \) which we then add to the flat rate amount of $50, which gives us a total bill of $60: \[ 50 + \color{red}{10}\\ = $60 \] And here is the full sequence of steps: \[ 3GB = 50 + (10 \cdot (\color{red}{3 - 2}\\ )) = 50 + (\color{red}{10 \cdot 1}\\ ) = \color{red}{50 + 10}\\ = $60\]
    With \( \color{red}{4GB} \) of data usage we subtract the flat rate of 2GB from the 4GB used which gives us a difference of 2GB: \[ \color{red}{4GB}\\ - 2GB = 2GB \] Then multiply the difference of \(\color{red}{2}\\ \) by the extra GB usage rate of $10: \[ \color{red}{2}\\ \cdot 10 = $20 \] Which gives us a product of \(\color{red}{$20}\\ \) which we then add to the flat rate amount of $50, which gives us a total bill of $70: \[ 50 + \color{red}{20}\\ = $70 \] And here is the full sequence of steps: \[ 4GB = 50 + (10 \cdot (\color{red}{4 - 2}\\ )) = 50 + (\color{red}{10 \cdot 2}\\ ) = \color{red}{50 + 20}\\ = $70\]
    With \( \color{red}{5GB} \) of data usage we subtract the flat rate of 2GB from the 5GB used which gives us a difference of 3GB: \[ \color{red}{5GB}\\ - 2GB = 3GB \] Then multiply the difference of \(\color{red}{3}\\ \) by the extra GB usage rate of $10: \[ \color{red}{3}\\ \cdot 10 = $30 \] Which gives us a product of \(\color{red}{$30}\\ \) which we then add to the flat rate amount of $50, which gives us a total bill of $80: \[ 50 + \color{red}{30}\\ = $80 \] And here is the full sequence of steps: \[ 5GB = 50 + (10 \cdot (\color{red}{5 - 2}\\ )) = 50 + (\color{red}{10 \cdot 3}\\ ) = \color{red}{50 + 30}\\ = $70\]
