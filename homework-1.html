2. Cell Phone Company

A cell phone company charges $50 per month for to 2GB of data. If you use more than 2GB of data, you will pay an extra $10 for each GB of data used beyond the included 2GB. For example, if you use 6GB of data, you will pay the base rate of $50, plus $40 ($10 for each of the 4 extra GB of data used), for a total of $90.

  1. How much would your monthly bill be if you use 0.5GB of data? What about 1GB of data? What about 2GB of data?

  3. How much would your monthly bill be if you use 3GB of data? What about 4GB of data? What about 5GB of data?
  4. Use your answers to (c) to find a function \(g(x)\) that gives the total cost of your monthly bill if you use \(x\) GB of data, where \(x \le 2\) (in other words, you use more than 2 GB of data.)
  5. Combine your answers to (b) and (d) to create a piecewise function that gives the monthly cost of using any amount of data.
